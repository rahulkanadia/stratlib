## 📖 Strategy Categories Explained

### Gap Strategies 🌅
**What**: Trade price gaps between yesterday's close and today's open
**When**: Market open
**Example**: Stock closed at $100, opens at $102 (gap up)

### Dip Buying Strategies 📉
**What**: Buy when price drops by historical average
**When**: Intraday dips
**Example**: Price usually drops $1 on red days, buy at -$1

### Two Indicator Strategies 🎯
**What**: Combine two signals for confirmation
**When**: Both conditions met
**Example**: RSI oversold + price below Bollinger Band

### High Frequency Strategies ⚡
**What**: Multiple trades per day
**When**: Quick opportunities
**Example**: 5-minute moving average crosses

### Volatility Strategies 📊
**What**: Trade based on market fear (VIX)
**When**: Specific volatility ranges
**Example**: Only trade when VIX between 15-25

## 📊 Understanding Results

### Key Metrics Explained

**Total Trades** 📈
- How many times the strategy bought/sold
- More trades = more active strategy
- Fewer trades = more selective strategy

**Win Rate** 🎯
- Percentage of profitable trades
- Above 50% is good
- Some strategies win less but profit more per win

**Total Profit/Loss** 💰
- Money made/lost starting with $10,000
- Positive = profitable strategy
- Compare to buy-and-hold

**Sharpe Ratio** 📐
- Risk-adjusted returns
- Above 1.0 = Good
- Above 2.0 = Excellent
- Measures consistency

**Max Drawdown** 📉
- Worst loss from peak
- -10% means at worst you'd be down 10%
- Lower is better (less risk)

**Win/Loss Ratio** ⚖️
- Average win size vs average loss size
- Above 1.0 = wins are bigger than losses
- High ratio can offset low win rate

### Interpreting Results

🟢 **Good Strategy Example**:
```
Total Trades: 85
Win Rate: 58%
Total Profit: $4,500
Sharpe Ratio: 1.8
Max Drawdown: -6%
Win/Loss Ratio: 1.5
```
→ Consistent, profitable, low risk

🟡 **Aggressive Strategy Example**:

#### 1. Change Lookback Period
```python

self.lookback_days = 21  # Change to 10, 30, etc.
```

#### 2. Adjust Gap Size
```python
# In gap strategies:
self.min_gap_percent = 0.5  # Change to 0.3, 1.0, etc.
```

#### 3. Modify RSI Levels
```python
# In RSI strategies:
self.rsi_overbought = 70  # Try 65, 75, 80
self.rsi_oversold = 30    # Try 25, 35, 40
```

```python
strategy.run_backtest(initial_capital=10000, position_size=0.5)  # Use 50% of capital
```
