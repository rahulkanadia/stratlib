# Research Methodology

## Hypothesis

Daily gaps in SPY (and similar ETFs) exhibit mean reversion behavior — when the market opens significantly above or below the previous close, it tends to "fill the gap" and return toward the prior close within the same trading day.

## Phase 1: Base Strategy Discovery (2009–2024)

1. Loaded 15+ years of SPY daily OHLCV data
2. Defined gap criteria: opening price deviates from previous close by at least $0.20, with the gap representing 15–85% of the prior day's range
3. Backtested simple mean reversion: go long on gap-down days, go short on gap-up days, exit at gap closure or day's close
4. Result: ~68% win rate, consistent profitability

## Phase 2: Volatility Filtering

1. Added **VIX filter** — only trade when VIX is within specific bounds (optimal: 13–22)
2. Added **ATR filter** — avoid trading during extreme volatility (ATR threshold optimization)
3. Two-step optimization: first optimize VIX range, then optimize ATR within VIX-filtered trades
4. Used multiprocessing grid search across 1000+ parameter combinations

## Phase 3: Sentiment & Indicator Overlay

1. **CPCI (Commitment of Traders)** — Tested sentiment ranges for trade selection
2. **RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands** — Technical indicator confirmation filters
3. **Moving Average Crossovers** — Trend direction filters
4. **Volume Spikes** — Liquidity confirmation
5. **Consecutive Day Patterns** — 2–3 red/green day setups

## Phase 4: Intraday Refinement

1. Added 30-minute, 5-minute, and 1-minute candle data for precise entry timing
2. Tested delayed entry (wait for first 30-min candle to confirm direction)
3. Built high-frequency strategies using sub-daily data

## Phase 5: Machine Learning

1. **Random Forest** — Trained on CPCI, VIX, ATR features to classify gap outcomes
2. **Gradient Boosting** — Ensemble approach for probability-weighted position sizing

## Phase 6: Validation

1. **Walk-Forward Analysis** — Train on N years, test on next year, roll forward
2. **Out-of-Sample 2025 Testing** — Forward-tested top strategies on unseen 2025 data
3. **Sharpe Ratio Improvement** — 2025 results showed 94% Sharpe improvement vs. backtest
4. **QQQ Cross-Validation** — Applied same strategies to QQQ, which outperformed SPY by 45%

## Overfitting Prevention

- Strategies had to be profitable in every single year (2000–2025) — no cherry-picking
- Walk-forward windows prevented look-ahead bias
- Parameter ranges kept wide (not over-fitted to narrow conditions)
- Out-of-sample testing on 2025 data was the final gate
